First off, Mitosis...

Over the last few weeks of class, Dan Shiffman's Mitosis Simulator was a challenge I'd accepted to understand and present to the class with my group members. It was a fun activity as I got to see Daniel Shiffman's workflow and I definitely have a goal to try and hit - being more comfortable with my workflow to the point where something as small as the mitosis simulator could take me 5 minutes to code and understand from scratch, rather than many weeks of work.

Outcasts: Trials of Light

By far the biggest achievement in this class was actually making a fun, and almost fully functional game. Using RPGMaker MV, we made an RPG about interestingly unique characters finding their paths to the light, with a goal to save the world from the darkness that veils its beauty.

Other Achievements and the Future

Things like embedded microcontrollers, twitter bot, and shared canvas were all bonding experiences for our group, while also learning lots about what's going on in the circuitry of a bot, applying laws of physics, and learning more about the command prompts of our computers, and how powerful they really are. When it comes to other things I want to do in the future, related to this course, is better my workflow. I used Git here and there, text editors like Atom and Processing 3 all the time, and GitHub every single day, but I want to become so used to all these tools that I can be like Dan Shiffman. One way on doing so is to continue coding his challenges, and staying updated weith his uploads, to keep up with what he posts and wants to share with the coding community on a daily basis.
